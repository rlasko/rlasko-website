Internship at Apple

Summer 2016
Overview
Before I arrived at Carnegie Mellon, I had never programmed before. A few weeks after class started, I began working for a psychology professor as a research engineer on his project. 4 months later I landed a software engineering internship with the Apple notes team
Unfortunately, the specifics of the work I did at Apple is confidential, though I'm able to share general information.
My internship lasted 11 weeks and consisted of working on an experimental project. Though I was primarily responsible for the project, I worked closely with the other 8 members of my team.

Scope
I was given an open-ended feature-level problem to solve through research, prototyping, and a full implementation into the Notes codebase.

Process
I completed a competitve analysis and did user research, including interviews as well assessing online reviews and the complaints log. Then I prototyped the system on paper, soliciting feedback from team members and other interns. I was limited to these groups due to confidentiality rules.
Throughout the process, I made both design decisions regarding how the user interacted with the feature and their overall mental model of it. The system architecture and protocol design I used ultimately affects the ability of my team members to later access and modify the code that I wrote. The overall design will be a critical consideration when evaluating my feature for release.
Contains process work from the internship
Completion
At the end of the 11 week internship, I finished implementing the first version of my feature, and made modifications through the standard review process. I also presented my final product and process work to a Senior Director and many managers in my department.
